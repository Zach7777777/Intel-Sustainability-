THROUGH THE AGES

Explore Intel's journey through time, discovering how our commitment to innovation has shaped a more sustainable future for technology and our planet.

1968

Intel Founded

The birth of Intel marked the beginning of the semiconductor revolution. Founded by Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore, Intel started with a vision to create memory solutions.
🌱 Early Foundation: Established principles of innovation and responsible manufacturing that would later evolve into comprehensive sustainability practices.
1971

First Microprocessor

The Intel 4004, the world's first microprocessor, revolutionized computing by putting the power of a computer on a single chip, enabling more efficient and compact designs.
⚡ Energy Efficiency: The 4004's compact design reduced power consumption and material usage, setting the foundation for energy-efficient computing.
1978

8086 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 processor with 32-bit architecture, ushering in a new era of performance and multitasking for personal computers.
🔄 Performance Per Watt: Advanced architecture delivered more computing power while maintaining energy efficiency, reducing the environmental impact per calculation.
2025

Net-Zero Commitment

Intel's comprehensive sustainability strategy focuses on achieving net-zero greenhouse gas emissions by 2040 and creating more sustainable products and processes.
🌍 RISE Strategy: Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, and Enabling technology for a better world. Zero waste to landfill by 2030, 100% renewable energy, and AI for sustainability initiatives.
